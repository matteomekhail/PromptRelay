# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Open-source maintainers who need AI assistance for their projects (code review, docs, tests, bugfixes) but lack time or compute. Developer volunteers who have local AI setups and want to contribute to OSS by running tasks on behalf of maintainers.

Both audiences are technical, skeptical of platforms that collect credentials, and value transparency.

## Product Purpose

PromptRelay is a volunteer AI execution network. Maintainers post public AI tasks; volunteers approve and run them locally using their own AI setup. No credential sharing, no server-side execution. The platform coordinates work, not compute.

Success looks like: a thriving exchange where maintainers get high-quality AI-generated results (reviews, diffs, docs) without sharing API keys, and volunteers contribute meaningfully to open source.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, credible, professional.

Quiet confidence. The kind of tool senior engineers recommend to each other. Not loud, not playful. Earned authority through transparency and technical rigor.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages (gradient blobs, stock illustrations, "Get Started Free" buttons)
- Crypto/web3 aesthetics (neon, dark purple, futuristic chrome)
- Purple gradients of any kind
- Overly playful or cartoon-y developer tools
- Cluttered dashboards trying to show everything at once

## Design Principles

1. **Earned trust through transparency.** Every claim is backed by a visible mechanism. Show how the system works, don't just assert safety.
2. **Developer-native confidence.** Speak the language of engineers. No marketing fluff. Technical precision over excitement.
3. **Quiet authority.** Apple-level restraint. Let whitespace, typography, and structure do the work. Never shout.
4. **Show the network.** The product is a coordination layer between people. Make that relationship visible.
5. **Respect the reader.** No repetition, no filler, no hand-holding. Dense, clear, scannable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Sufficient color contrast ratios. No information conveyed by color alone.
